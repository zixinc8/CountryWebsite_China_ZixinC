History of Chinese Dynasties

夏商与西周,东周分两段;
春秋和战国,一统秦两汉;
三分魏蜀吴,二晋前后延;

The Xia, Shang, and Western Zhou dynasties were followed by the Eastern Zhou dynasty, which marked the beginning of a period of fragmentation.
After the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods, the Qin Dynasty unified China, followed by the Western Han and Eastern Han dynasties.
The Three Kingdoms period saw the states of Wei, Shu, and Wu in a tripartite balance of power, followed by the Western Jin and Eastern Jin dynasties.
The Northern and Southern Dynasties were in a state of conflict, followed by the unification under the Sui Dynasty, then the Tang Dynasty, and subsequently the Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period.
The Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties, with the Qing dynasty being the last, marked the end of China's ancient imperial system.
